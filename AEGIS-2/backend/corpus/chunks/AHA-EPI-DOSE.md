# Epinephrine Dosing in Adult Cardiac Arrest

The recommended dose of epinephrine in adult cardiac arrest is 1 mg IV
or IO, repeated every 3 to 5 minutes. The earliest reasonable
administration depends on rhythm:

- **Non-shockable rhythm (asystole, PEA):** administer epinephrine as
  soon as IV/IO access is established. Earlier administration is
  associated with improved survival and neurologically intact recovery.
- **Shockable rhythm (VF, pulseless VT):** administer after the second
  defibrillation attempt has failed to convert the rhythm. Defibrillation
  takes precedence; do not delay shocks for vasopressors.

Higher-dose epinephrine (5 mg) is no longer recommended for routine use
in adult cardiac arrest. Vasopressin is no longer recommended as a
substitute for or in combination with epinephrine.

Document each dose, time, and route on the resuscitation record.
Continued absence of ROSC after multiple cycles requires reassessment
for reversible causes (the H's and T's).
