# Drowning Resuscitation

Cardiac arrest from drowning is hypoxic in origin. Oxygen depletion is
the primary mechanism; cardiac electrical dysfunction is secondary. The
resuscitation sequence reflects this:

1. Remove the casualty from the water and place them on a firm,
   non-conductive surface. Do not delay rescue breaths to reach an
   ideal location if the casualty is already supine on a flat surface.
2. **Deliver 5 initial rescue breaths** — this is the key deviation
   from standard adult BLS, where compressions begin first. In drowning
   arrest, oxygenation precedes circulation.
3. Begin compressions at 100–120/min if no signs of life return after
   the initial breaths.
4. Continue with 30:2 compression-to-ventilation ratio until advanced
   airway is in place; then continuous compressions with asynchronous
   ventilations at 1 breath every 6 seconds.

AED application is appropriate as soon as the casualty is dry. The
hypoxic etiology means most drowning arrests present in non-shockable
rhythms initially; absence of "shock advised" does not prognosticate.
