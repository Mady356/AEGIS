# Tranexamic Acid Dosing

Tranexamic acid (TXA) is indicated for any casualty with significant
hemorrhage or who is anticipated to require blood transfusion.
Administration must begin within 3 hours of injury — efficacy declines
sharply after this window and may be harmful beyond it.

**Adult dose:** 2 g loading dose, mixed in 100 mL of normal saline,
administered as a slow IV push or short infusion over 10 minutes. A
second 1 g dose may be given as an infusion over 8 hours if ongoing
hemorrhage is suspected.

**Contraindications:**
- Concurrent anticoagulant therapy (warfarin, DOACs)
- Known thromboembolic disease
- Time since injury exceeding 3 hours

**Administration site:** antecubital fossa with an 18-gauge catheter is
preferred. Do NOT administer TXA in the same line as blood products
or hypertonic saline; flush with normal saline before and after.

Document time of administration on the casualty card alongside dose
and route.
