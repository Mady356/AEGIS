# AED Use Sequence

When an AED arrives, apply pads as soon as the casualty is on a dry,
non-conductive surface. Do not pause compressions to set up the AED if
another rescuer is available; only pause when the AED prompts for
rhythm analysis.

Pad placement (adult): one pad on the upper right chest below the
clavicle, the second pad on the lower left chest at the mid-axillary
line. For pediatric patients under 8 years or 25 kg, use pediatric pads
or a pediatric attenuator if available; if not, adult pads may be used,
placed anterior-posterior.

When the AED indicates "shock advised":
1. Clear the casualty — confirm no rescuer is in contact
2. Deliver the shock
3. Resume compressions immediately — do not perform a pulse check
4. Continue 2-minute cycles until rhythm reanalysis or ROSC

If "no shock advised," resume compressions immediately and continue
2-minute cycles. Reanalyze with the AED at the end of each cycle.
