# TCCC Casualty Card Documentation

Every casualty receives a TCCC Casualty Card (DD Form 1380) completed by
the first medical responder. The card stays with the casualty through every
echelon of care.

Required elements:

- Casualty name and unit (or unique identifier if anonymized)
- Battle roster number or service member ID
- Mechanism of injury and time of wounding
- Vital signs at the time of treatment, with timestamps
- All interventions performed, with timestamps
- Medications administered: name, dose, route, time
- **Tourniquets:** location, time of placement, and "TQ" written on the
  casualty's forehead in indelible marker so subsequent providers can
  identify the limb at risk without unwrapping the casualty.
- Provider's name and time of evaluation

Documentation is a clinical priority, not a clerical one. Lost or absent
documentation forces downstream providers to make decisions without
critical context, which has been associated with adverse outcomes in
multiple cohort reviews.
