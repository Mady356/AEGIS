# Pediatric Weight Estimation

Accurate weight is essential for pediatric drug dosing. In order of
preference:

1. **Actual measured weight** — use a scale whenever the patient is
   stable enough to be weighed. Even a brief reading is preferable to
   estimation.
2. **Length-based resuscitation tape** (Broselow or equivalent) — the
   standard for emergency settings; estimates weight from length and
   provides medication doses on a single reference.
3. **Age-based formula** — use only when neither of the above is
   available, and acknowledge the wider error margin:
   - Infants 1–12 months: weight (kg) = (age in months + 9) / 2
   - Children 1–10 years: weight (kg) = 2 × (age + 4)

Worked examples (age-based):
- 6-month-old: (6 + 9) / 2 = 7.5 kg
- 4-year-old: 2 × (4 + 4) = 16 kg
- 8-year-old: 2 × (8 + 4) = 24 kg

Document weight method (measured / tape / age-formula) on the triage
tag alongside the value. Errors of 30%+ are common with age-only
estimation in older children, especially in undernourished populations.
