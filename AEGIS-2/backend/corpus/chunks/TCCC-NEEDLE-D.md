# Needle Decompression for Tension Pneumothorax

Indications: chest trauma with progressive respiratory distress, tracheal
deviation, absent breath sounds on the affected side, or hemodynamic
deterioration suggestive of tension physiology.

**Procedure (anterior site, primary):** locate the 5th intercostal space at
the anterior axillary line on the affected side. Cleanse the site. Insert
a 14-gauge, 3.25-inch (8 cm) catheter perpendicular to the chest wall,
advancing over the superior margin of the 6th rib to avoid the
neurovascular bundle running along the inferior margin of the 5th.

A successful decompression is signaled by an audible release of air,
improvement in respiratory effort, or restoration of hemodynamics.
Leave the catheter in place; secure with tape. Reassess every 5 minutes;
needle decompression may need to be repeated as the catheter occludes
or kinks.

**Secondary site:** 2nd intercostal space at the midclavicular line if the
anterior axillary site is unsuitable due to wounds or armor.
