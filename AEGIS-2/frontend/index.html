    System Diagnostics v2.0.0
    Trust and Scope
    AEGIS is a documentation interface, not a clinical advisor. The local LLM extracts, retrieves, and summarizes — it does not prescribe.

    LLM Jobs

    1. ExtractionVoice transcript → structured facts
    2. Reference QAOperator question → cited answer or refusal
    3. NudgesEncounter state → protocol-cited reminders
    4. After-actionCompleted encounter → cited review

    Refusal Behavior

    • The LLM refuses when retrieved corpus does not support an answer.
    • Refusals are logged and visible in the encounter record.

    Demo Fallbacks

    • Three pre-recorded voice clips (Ctrl+Shift+1/2/3) route through the live Whisper pipeline.

    Cryptographic Guarantees

    • SHA-256 integrity chain on all encounter events
    • Ed25519 device signatures on handoff packets
    • All keys generated locally on first run; no keys leave the device

    Deferred to Future Versions

    • Multi-patient encounter queue · camera-based vital estimation
    • Drug interaction database · mesh networking between AEGIS units

    Who AEGIS Is For BRIEF

    Maria Chen is an EMT in rural Colorado, 70 minutes from the nearest Level III trauma center. Last winter she responded to a snowmobile crash in a canyon with no cellular coverage. Her patient had a suspected pelvic fracture and she had thirty minutes of training on that specific injury, two years earlier. She made the call alone. She did okay. She still thinks about whether she got it right.

    AEGIS exists for the next time Maria takes that call.
