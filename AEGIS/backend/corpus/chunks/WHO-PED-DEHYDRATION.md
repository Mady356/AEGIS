# Pediatric Dehydration Classification

Classify dehydration severity by clinical signs:

**Severe dehydration** (any TWO of):
- Lethargic or unconscious
- Sunken eyes
- Unable to drink or drinks poorly
- Skin pinch goes back very slowly (≥ 2 seconds)

→ Immediate IV/IO fluids per Plan C; evaluate for shock.

**Some dehydration** (any TWO of):
- Restless, irritable
- Sunken eyes
- Drinks eagerly, thirsty
- Skin pinch goes back slowly (1–2 seconds)

→ Oral rehydration solution per Plan B; reassess at 60 minutes.

**No dehydration:**
- None of the above

→ Continue normal feeding; supplement with extra fluids; reassess if
clinical condition changes.

Capillary refill time is a useful adjunct: refill > 3 seconds suggests
some dehydration; > 4 seconds with altered mental status suggests
severe dehydration.

Reassess every 60 minutes for the first 4 hours, then every 4 hours
thereafter. Escalate the classification at any reassessment that meets
new criteria.
