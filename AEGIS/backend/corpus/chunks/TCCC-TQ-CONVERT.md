# Tourniquet Conversion

Conversion of a tourniquet to a pressure dressing may be attempted only
when ALL of the following criteria are met:

1. The casualty is hemodynamically stable (palpable radial pulse, mental
   status preserved or returning, systolic blood pressure ≥ 90 mmHg).
2. Total tourniquet time is less than 2 hours.
3. The tactical situation permits — the casualty is in a secured area with
   sustained provider attention.
4. The wound is not a result of major junctional vessel injury.
5. The casualty is not in shock and has not received massive transfusion.

If any criterion is unmet, the tourniquet remains in place and the
casualty is moved to a higher level of care for surgical evaluation.

When conversion is attempted, loosen the windlass slowly. Maintain direct
pressure on the wound throughout. Reassess for re-bleeding for at least
2 minutes before declaring the conversion successful.
