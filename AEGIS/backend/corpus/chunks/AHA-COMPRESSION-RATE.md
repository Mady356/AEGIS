# Adult Compression Rate and Depth

Deliver chest compressions at a rate of 100 to 120 per minute. Compress
the lower half of the sternum to a depth of at least 5 cm (2 inches) but
not more than 6 cm (2.4 inches) in the average adult. Allow complete
chest recoil between compressions; leaning on the chest reduces preload
and is associated with worse outcomes.

Minimize interruptions in compressions. The chest compression fraction —
the proportion of time during arrest in which compressions are being
delivered — should exceed 60%, and the goal in well-resourced settings
is over 80%. Pauses for rhythm analysis, shock delivery, and (rarely)
intubation should be as brief as possible.

When two rescuers are available, switch the compressor every 2 minutes
or sooner if quality degrades. Quality degrades faster than the
compressor recognizes; use a metronome or AED audio guidance when
available.
