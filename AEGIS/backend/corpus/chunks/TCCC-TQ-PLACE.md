# Tourniquet Placement

For life-threatening external hemorrhage, apply a CAT (Combat Application
Tourniquet) or equivalent limb tourniquet 2–3 inches (5–7 cm) proximal to
the bleeding wound, on a single bone segment when possible.

For massive hemorrhage where the bleeding source is not immediately visible,
or for traumatic amputation, apply "high and tight" — that is, as proximal
on the limb as possible, directly to skin, over the femoral or brachial
artery.

Tighten the windlass until both of the following conditions are confirmed:
the distal pulse is absent, and visible bleeding has stopped. Either
condition alone is insufficient. If bleeding persists after the first
tourniquet, place a second tourniquet directly above the first.

Do not place a tourniquet over a joint. Do not place a tourniquet over an
external pocket containing items that would compress unevenly into the limb.
