# The MARCH Algorithm

All combat casualty assessments follow the MARCH-PAWS sequence. The first
five steps establish life-saving priorities in order:

- **M — Massive hemorrhage.** Identify and stop life-threatening
  external bleeding. Tourniquets and hemostatic dressings as indicated.
- **A — Airway.** Open and maintain a patent airway. Position, suction,
  and adjunct as needed.
- **R — Respiration.** Identify and treat tension pneumothorax with
  needle decompression. Manage open chest wounds with vented chest seals.
- **C — Circulation.** Assess perfusion. Establish IV/IO access if
  shock is present. Initiate fluid resuscitation per protocol.
- **H — Hypothermia / Head injury.** Prevent body heat loss. Document
  any head injury and its severity for the casualty card.

The order is non-negotiable for the first three steps: massive hemorrhage
takes precedence over airway when arterial bleeding is actively visible,
because uncontrolled bleeding kills before airway compromise does in the
typical combat casualty.

PAWS — Pain control, Antibiotics, Wounds, Splints — follows once MARCH
is completed.
