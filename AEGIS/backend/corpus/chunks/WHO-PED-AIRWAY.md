# Pediatric Airway Management — Anatomical Adjustments

Pediatric airway anatomy differs from adult in ways that affect
positioning and adjunct selection:

- **Larger relative tongue and tonsils** — more prone to airway
  obstruction during reduced level of consciousness
- **More cephalad larynx** (C3–C4 vs C5–C6 in adults) — straight blade
  often preferred for direct laryngoscopy in children under 3
- **Funnel-shaped airway** narrowest at the cricoid ring (until ~age
  10) — uncuffed tubes have historically been preferred, though current
  guidelines accept cuffed tubes with low cuff pressures (< 20 cmH₂O)
- **Larger occiput** — neutral head position is achieved with a
  shoulder roll rather than head extension; aggressive head extension
  obstructs the airway in infants

ETT sizing:
- Cuffed: (age in years / 4) + 3.5
- Uncuffed: (age in years / 4) + 4

For a 4-year-old: cuffed 4.5 mm, uncuffed 5.0 mm. Confirm placement
with bilateral breath sounds and end-tidal CO₂.

Use length-based tape (Broselow or equivalent) for both ETT sizing and
weight estimation when accurate weight is not available.
