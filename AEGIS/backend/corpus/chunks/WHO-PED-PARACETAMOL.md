# Pediatric Paracetamol Dosing

Paracetamol (acetaminophen) is dosed by weight, not by age:

- **Single dose:** 15 mg/kg orally every 4–6 hours
- **Maximum daily dose:** 60 mg/kg per day (not to exceed 4 g/day in
  any pediatric patient)
- **Avoid in:** known hepatic impairment, suspected ingestion of
  hepatotoxic substances, severely dehydrated state pending fluid
  resuscitation

**Worked example, 16 kg child:**
- Single dose: 16 × 15 = **240 mg PO**
- Volume of standard 120 mg / 5 mL suspension: 240 ÷ 24 = **10 mL**

For non-oral routes, IV paracetamol may be used at the same
mg/kg dose; rectal suppositories are an alternative when oral intake
is not tolerated.

Document weight (measured or estimated by length-based tape) on the
triage tag before any dose is administered. Do not estimate weight
by age alone; the pediatric weight band is too wide for that to be
safe in dosing decisions.
