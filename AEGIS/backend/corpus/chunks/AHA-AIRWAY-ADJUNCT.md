# Airway Adjunct Selection (OPA vs NPA)

Choose between an oropharyngeal airway (OPA) and a nasopharyngeal airway
(NPA) based on the casualty's level of consciousness and gag reflex:

- **OPA** is appropriate for casualties without a gag reflex (typically
  unresponsive, no response to airway stimulation). Sizing: measure from
  the corner of the casualty's mouth to the angle of the mandible.
  Insertion: in adults, insert with the curve toward the palate, then
  rotate 180° as the tip passes the soft palate. In children, insert
  with the curve already in the anatomical orientation using a tongue
  depressor.

- **NPA** is appropriate for casualties with an intact gag reflex or
  partially responsive states. NPA is contraindicated in suspected
  basilar skull fracture (CSF rhinorrhea, raccoon eyes, Battle's sign).
  Sizing: measure from the tip of the nostril to the tragus of the ear.
  Lubricate generously. Insert into the larger nostril with the bevel
  toward the septum.

Reassess airway patency continuously after adjunct placement; an
adjunct does not replace ongoing positioning and suctioning.
