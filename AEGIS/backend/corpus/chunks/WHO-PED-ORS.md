# Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)

Composition (low-osmolarity ORS, the WHO standard):
- Sodium chloride: 2.6 g/L
- Potassium chloride: 1.5 g/L
- Trisodium citrate dihydrate: 2.9 g/L
- Glucose anhydrous: 13.5 g/L
- Total osmolarity: ≈ 245 mOsm/L

**Plan B dosing:** 75 mL/kg over 4 hours, given as small frequent sips.
Total volume for a 16 kg child: 1.2 L over 4 hours, i.e. ~5 mL/min.

If vomiting occurs, pause administration for 10 minutes, then resume
at a slower rate. Persistent vomiting (more than 2 episodes during the
4-hour window) is an indication to escalate to IV fluid resuscitation
per Plan C.

After 4 hours, reassess hydration status. If the patient has improved
to "no dehydration," continue maintenance fluids and discharge with
ORS at home. If still in "some dehydration," repeat Plan B for another
4 hours. If progressed to "severe dehydration," escalate to Plan C.

ORS is not effective for managing the febrile illness itself; it
addresses the volume status only. Treat the underlying illness in
parallel.
