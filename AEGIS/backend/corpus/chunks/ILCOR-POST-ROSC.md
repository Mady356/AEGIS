# Post-ROSC Management Priorities

After return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC), the immediate
priorities are:

1. **Airway and ventilation.** Confirm advanced airway placement (if
   placed) with continuous capnography. Ventilate to a normal end-tidal
   CO₂ of 35–45 mmHg. Avoid hyperventilation; it reduces cerebral
   perfusion in this state.
2. **Oxygenation.** Titrate FiO₂ to maintain SpO₂ between 92% and 98%.
   Hyperoxia is associated with worse neurological outcomes.
3. **Circulation.** Target a mean arterial pressure ≥ 65 mmHg. Use
   crystalloid boluses cautiously; pressors (norepinephrine first-line)
   are preferred to large-volume resuscitation in the post-arrest state.
4. **Targeted temperature management.** All comatose post-ROSC casualties
   should receive temperature management with a target between 32°C and
   36°C for at least 24 hours.
5. **12-lead ECG and reversible-cause workup.** Rule out STEMI, hyper- /
   hypokalemia, hypovolemia, hypoxia, hydrogen ion (acidosis), tamponade,
   tension pneumothorax, thrombosis (pulmonary or coronary), toxins.

Document the time of ROSC. Recurrent arrest within minutes of ROSC is
common; maintain a heightened state of readiness.
